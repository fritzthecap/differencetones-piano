See and Hear Difference-Tones Generated by Various Intervals

Activate "Hold" so that any clicked piano key will create a constant sound. Then click a piano key in some upper octave. The configured "Autoplay Interval" tone (above the clicked one) will be added automatically. These two interval tones will be marked by a red border. The resulting difference-tone will be marked on the piano by its selection color and a red border (in case it is in the keyboard's range). Although it looks like, it is not played on the piano, it sounds through the interval tones!

To stop the sound, click "Hold" or one of the two piano keys once again. You can change any parameter on top while the sound plays.

Try to change the "Autoplay Interval" to MINOR_THIRD while the sound plays and watch where the red bordered difference-tone goes on the piano.

When you reduce the "Deviation Tolerance" while playing, you will see that the red bordered difference-tone disappears at some level.

Change the "Tuning" from EqualTemperament to some JustIntonation tuning. You will hear less beatings, because EqualTemperament is not a "pure" tuning.
