Interval Cent Distances

We have three major thirds in a C-major scale, [C-E], [F-A] and [G-B]. The question rises: has [C-E] the same Cent-distance as [F-A], and has [F-A] the same as [G-B]? This question rises for every interval.

For EqualTemperament, all Cent-distances are the same, because all semitones have a distance of 100 Cent. Its frequencies are calculated by the formula

frequency = frequencyOfA4 * (12th root of 2 power semitoneOffsetFromA4)

In fraction-based tunings it is different, because the Cent-distances depend on integer-fractions. Some chords will sound out-of-tune in JustIntonation, while others will sound better than in EqualTemperament (less beatings). This checker app helps to find out which intervals and triads will sound dirty.
